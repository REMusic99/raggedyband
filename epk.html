BRIEF BIO
Acoustic/Electric Duo Bob Farley and Lynn Perdzock Bring Jazzy Folk Rock to Life
Singer-songwriters Bob Farley and Lynn Perdzock captivate audiences nationwide with their unique blend of jazzy folk rock. Known for their upbeat, positive energy, the duo has penned and performed a diverse repertoire that spans heartfelt love songs to raucous rock ballads. Infused with humor and authenticity, their music reflects their deep connection to each other and the world around them, offering a refreshing and engaging listening experience for music lovers across the country.

BEEFIER BIO
Raggedy Edge: A Duo Blending Rock, Americana, and Storytelling
When solo artists Bob Farley and Lynn Perdzock teamed up, they quickly discovered a shared passion for crafting music that bridges genres. Combining Farley's rock influences—The Beatles, Clapton, Hendrix, and Led Zeppelin—with Perdzock's love for heartfelt ballads and Americana--Joni Mitchell, Kate Bush, Bonnie Raitt-- the pair created Raggedy Edge, a name that reflects their seamless journey through diverse musical styles.

Known for their "upbeat, laid-back positive vibe," Raggedy Edge showcases their versatility on guitar, mandolin, ukulele, and percussion, with Bob adding harmonica during live performances and keyboards in the studio.

The duo debuted in 2012 with With You, a collection of story-driven love songs and blues-tinged humor, exemplified by the comedic twist in "Two Left Feet." Three more albums followed in 2014, 2016, and 2019, along with two EPs released between their later works. Fans can find their music on all major platforms, though the duo focuses on connecting through their Raggedy Edge Bandcamp page, where their evolving sound continues to resonate with listeners nationwide.

STAGE REQUIREMENTS
The larger the stage, the better, but as a duo, Raggedy Edge can fit into a space as small as 10 feet by 6 feet. That accommodates their 10-channel mixer and a small amplifier. When there is a house system, their preference is to run their mix to the house system. Power-wise, one unused 120-amp circuit provides plenty of juice.
