Meeting
Lynn Perdzock met Bob Farley in Dayton, Ohio, at an open mic he was hosting. Lynn was searching for a guitarist, and Bob was hoping to find a vocalist who could play guitar or mandolin. A month later, they played their first show, blending covers and original music that sounded better together than ever before. Crowds agreed, and their journey began, crisscrossing the country to share their passion for music.

Raggedy Edge: Albums and Milestones
Encouraged by their musical friend Curtis Alley, the duo recorded their first album, With You, in Dayton. This was followed by My Favorite Deja Vu, recorded in Hurricane, Utah, featuring live bass, keys, and drums. Over the years, they’ve released two more albums— Where You Want to Be and Have Folk~Will Rock—as well as Bob’s solo project, Raggedy.

Adapting to Change
When COVID-19 upended live performances, Raggedy Edge transitioned to live streaming, connecting with new audiences across the country. They took the downtime to start fresh, trading Ohio's gray skies for Arizona's sunshine. Despite challenges like declining CD sales and rising touring costs, Lynn and Bob remain committed to adapting their lifestyle and approach to keep the music alive.

The Littlest Big Band
One fateful gig at an assisted living community inspired a new chapter for the duo. To better suit audiences enjoying the Great American Songbook, Lynn and Bob created The Littlest Big Band. This project showcases music from the 1920s through the 1970s and now keeps them busy with over 200 performances annually.

What’s Next
As time passes, Raggedy Edge continues to weave timeless classics into their performances alongside original songs. Though the industry keeps changing, their love for singing, playing, and connecting with audiences remains unwavering. Lynn and Bob are excited for what lies ahead, embracing every challenge as an opportunity to grow.
